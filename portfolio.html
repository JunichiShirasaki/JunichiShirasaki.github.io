CC10. Advanced Analysis and Machine Learning

The task in this code challenge is to produce an advanced analytics chart and conduct machine learning.
Hypothesis: After controlling for income, OECD countries differ in per-capita CO2 emissions, indicating the role of non-income factors such as energy mix and policy.

Chart17
The correlation between carbon dioxide emissions and GDP in OECD countries

This chart illustrates the relationship between GDP per capita and carbon dioxide emissions per capita among OECD countries in 2019. Overall, this suggests that a positive relationship between economic prosperity and per capita carbon dioxide emissions. However we can see that there is substantial variation around the regression line.

Chart18
De-trended CO2 emissions per capita

This chart shows de-trended CO2 emissions per capita. Positive values indicate countries emitting more CO2 than predicted by income, while negative values indicate under-performance.
From chart 17 and 18, I can find as follows:
Although higher income tends to increase CO2 emissions, larger differences remain across OECD countries after controlling for income, which may suggest the importance of energy policy choices.

Data Source from the World Bank: GDP per capita, CO2 emissions per capita
Data for GDP per capita and CO2 emissions per capita were retreived from the World Bank API.
The datasets were cleaned, merged and filtered to include OECD countries only in the year of 2019.
I used sklearn in the Colab Notebook below. The method is supervised machine learning (linear regression model)
X matrix is GDP per capita and Y vecor is CO2 emissions per capita.
